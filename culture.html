Dos & Taboos


Dos

  • It is recommended to accept any food or drink offered; common for hospitality.
  • Punctuality is appriciated, but most Filipinos tend to not be on time - especially in social informal gatherings.
  • Address someone with a high and respected position by their title.

Taboos

  • When in a conflict, it is not recommended to confront a colleague publicly. Feedback should be provided in a positive and constructive manner
  • Do not publically display your anger or irritation towards another person.
  • Steer away from talking about politics.

Global Ethics & Social Responsibility

Environment

As of recent, the Taal volcano erupted on January 12th, sending ash into the sky and lava onto the waters. It is located 70km south of Manila, the capital, which could threaten the city as earthquakes resulted from the eruption. It is expected that the volcano will continue to be active for the next few days. In addition, Taal sits on a large lake, which has a high possibly that the eruption can create a dangerous tsunami to towns and cities in the surrounding area.


Human Rights

President Duterte faces international criticism since he took office in 2016. The drug war he initiated caused the deaths of over 12 000 people. Homicide rates have also increased since the war began, as well as the attacks of journalists and news outlets that criticized his actions, violating the freedom of the press.


Corruption

There is a belief of widespread corruption in the Philippines that may hinder business in the country. Although it does run rampant with Duterte’s “war on drugs” campaign, Filipinos in general are moral and honest, where these values are commonly practiced.
